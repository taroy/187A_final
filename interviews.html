Interviews


We have listed some of the questions that we asked our interview subjects. We started with some questions about their relationship to the Norwegian folk costume in order to see if they would fit into one of our user groups. We also questioned them about what they would like to know about bunads.

1. Do you own a bunad?
1b. Where is it from and when did you get it?

2. Are you planning on getting a bunad in the future?

3. What do you like about bunads?

4. What do you know about the traditions that are associated with bunads?
4b. What would you like to know more about?

5. Have you ever looked at bunads online?
5b. Do you think that you would look for a bunad online?

6. Would you consider to sew your own bunad?


Experts


Hanne

Hanne owns a Romeriket bunad that her mom gave her for her Confirmation. She knows a lot about the bunad tradition. Her grandmother had a book about bunads and used to tell Hanne about them when Hanne was younger. Hanne also participated in a bunad parade as a kid. Some of her family friends are goldsmiths and make special bunad silver, so she got her silver from them. Hanne likes that you can always wear it in weddings, baptisms, the national day etc. And that she never has to worry about getting a dress.

Kirsten

Kirsten does not own a bunad and is not planning on buying one now, but she wishes she had one. She thinks bunads are very pretty and likes that you can use them over and over again. For Kirsten it's about tradition and origin and she finds it important to have a link to the bunad you own. Kirsten is concerned about preserving the heritage. She stated that a key issue is that they are handmade and not a mass produced. "It doesn't get more Norwegian than a bunad."

She also has a lot of knowledge about the tradition in general. For example that there are different silver jewelry that belongs to the different bunads and that back in the days it was a distinction between everyday clothing and festive clothing.

Kirsten has read about bunads online when she was looking for a bunad for her niece. The niece has family in three different parts of Norway, so Kirsten was comparing the various bunads to each other. She was looking at appearance, price and historical information about the bunads.

Elin

Elin owns a bunad from Nordland where she has family roots. It is important to her to have a bunad that is related to the origin of her family. She is very interested in the tradition and have read several books on the subject. Some years ago she looked up a bunad online. She considered sewing a Hallingdals bunad for one of her relatives and wanted to look closer at the details of that particular costume. The project were however postponed due to the high price.


Moderate


Jens

Jens does not own a bunad and are not planning on getting one. He thinks they are too warm, but he likes the way they look on others. Especially on women. He has never read about bunads online, but knows that they are Norways national costume based on regions and places in Norway, that it's a traditional festive clothing and elaborate handwork with special patterns and silver. Jens also knows that they are made of wool and are very hard to iron.

Thomas

Thomas does not own a bunad, but wants one. For now the expenses are too high, but he has already begun the process of deciding what kind of folk costume he wants. Thomas has been looking in a bunad encyclopedia and would be interested in searching the web as well. He is concerned with how the bunad looks and has so far focused on the images. He has been looking at all kinds of bunads, but admits that he will choose one from a place where he has relatives.

Elin Hasas

Elin is owner of a bunad from Vestfold, the part of Norway where she is born and raised. She inherited it from her mother when she was 14 years old and went through the sacrament of Confirmation. Elin has never looked at bunads online, but if she were to do it she would be looking for accessorize for the Vestfold bunad that she owns. Elin has some knowledge about the tradition, for example that girls are not supposed to wear a silver belt before they get married, but she would love to learn more about how the tradition started.


Novice


Anders

Anders does not own a bunad, but would like one if he looked good in it. He thinks bunads are very classy and nice, but that they seem kind of unpractical because they are so heavy and warm. He knows that there are many different bunads from different counties of Norway, that they are made of wool and have lots of silver, and some have special headgear and shoes. Anders has not looked at bunads online and would rather not sew one himself. "That would take way too much time."

Maren

Maren does not have a bunad, but she is starting to want one. She has never looked at a bunad online, but if she were to do so she would look at quality, price and where she could buy it. Maren thinks it is important to get a bunad from a place where she feels that she belongs or where she has family. She would rather want a medium nice looking bunad from a place where she belongs, than a more beautiful bunad from a place that she does not have any connections.

Corey

Corey is born and raised in the United States. When we asked him about the Norwegian folk costume bunad he had never heard of it before. Further we asked him what he would want to know about it now that he knows that such a traditional costume exists. Corey was most curious about how it looks and on what occasions Norwegians use it. He would like to go online to learn more about it.


Interview Results

After interviewing all the expert, moderate and novice users we found that the information they gave was as expected. The expert users all had a bunad or wished that they did. They had a lot of knowledge about bunads and were genuinely interested in its history and tradition. The moderate users either had a bunad, wanted a bunad or didn't want a bunad. All of them had general knowledge about bunads like many norwegians do. The novice users didn't own a bunad and they had some or no knowledge about them. We found that most of the users like bunads and thinks that it is important that the owner of a bunad has a connection to the place the bunad is from.
